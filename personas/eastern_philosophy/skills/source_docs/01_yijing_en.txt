The I Ching, or Book of Changes, is one of the oldest Chinese classics. Using 64 hexagrams, it reveals the laws of change governing the universe. Its core concepts are change, simplicity, and constancy. Each hexagram consists of six lines, and through the combination of yin and yang lines, it simulates the operating principles of all things. Confucius studied it so intensively in his later years that the leather binding wore out three times. The I Ching teaches that nothing is eternal except change itself. One should seize opportunities amid change, maintain hope in adversity, and prepare for danger in times of peace.
