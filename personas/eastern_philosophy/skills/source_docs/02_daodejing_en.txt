The Tao Te Ching, written by Laozi, is the foundational text of Chinese Taoist philosophy. In just 5,000 characters, it contains the highest wisdom on the universe, life, and governance. Its core concept is "Tao follows nature" - all things follow the operating principles of the Tao, whose essence is natural non-action. Laozi advocates "governing by non-interference," which means acting in accordance with natural laws, not forcing things. "The highest goodness is like water, which benefits all things without competing." Softness overcomes hardness, great wisdom appears foolish, and contentment is true wealth.
