The story of art is the story of humanity learning to see. Prehistoric people painted bison deep in caves not as decoration but as faith. Renaissance artists invented perspective, creating 3D illusion on 2D surface. The Impressionists captured fleeting light outdoors, teaching us the world is woven from light and color, not lines. Modern abstraction freed art from the mandate to represent, telling us emotion itself can be a painting. The history of art is not a story of technical advancement but of the repeated liberation of human perception.
