The basic elements of design - line, shape, color, texture, and space - form the fundamental language of all visual design. Line guides the eye. Shape occupies space. Color evokes emotion. Texture adds depth. Space creates breathing room. Mastering these elements is not about learning rules but cultivating a way of seeing. The next time you look at any object or scene, see not the object itself but how lines flow at its edges, how colors leap across surfaces, how space breathes between forms. This is the designer's eye.
