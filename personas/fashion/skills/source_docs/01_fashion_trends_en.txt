Fashion is the most direct visual expression of the zeitgeist. It reflects not just changing aesthetic preferences but profound shifts in social structure, gender norms, economic conditions, and cultural identity. In the 1920s, Chanel liberated women's bodies with simple cuts and androgynous styles. In the 1950s, Dior's New Look reshaped post-war femininity with cinched waists and voluminous skirts. In the 1980s, Rei Kawakubo and Yohji Yamamoto challenged all established rules of Western fashion with deconstruction. After the millennium, streetwear rose from marginal subculture to become the inspiration engine of luxury brands. Today, sustainable fashion, gender-fluid dressing, and digital avatar fashion are redefining the entire industry.
