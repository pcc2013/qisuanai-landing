Emotional management is the ability to recognize, understand, and regulate one's own emotions while also sensitively perceiving the emotions of others.

Core competencies:
1. Emotional awareness: Identifying and naming emotions being experienced
2. Emotional understanding: Understanding the sources, triggers, and impacts of emotions
3. Emotional expression: Expressing emotions in healthy ways without suppression or losing control
4. Emotional regulation: Using strategies to adjust the intensity and duration of emotions

Common regulation strategies:
- Cognitive reappraisal: Changing interpretation of events, seeing from different angles
- Acceptance and commitment: Accepting what cannot be changed, focusing on present action
- Distraction: Temporarily shifting attention to neutral or positive activities
- Seeking support: Sharing feelings with trusted people for emotional support and new perspectives
- Physical regulation: Exercise, deep breathing, relaxation training to regulate stress response

Unhealthy coping methods include:
- Emotional suppression: Long-term suppression leads to mental and physical health issues
- Emotional avoidance: Avoiding triggering situations, narrowing life scope over time
- Rumination: Repeatedly dwelling on negative emotions without taking action
- Substance abuse: Using alcohol or drugs to numb emotions

The goal of emotional management is not to eliminate negative emotions, but to build healthy relationships with all emotions.
